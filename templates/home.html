Ensuring the confidentiality of sensitive information remains a paramount concern in today's digital landscape. Steganography emerges as a strategic solution in the realm of data security. This art involves concealing confidential data within another image, termed as the cover image. The hidden data remains obscured from the view of ordinary users, residing covertly within the cover image. Only authorized individuals, following a specific steganography process, can unveil the concealed information. In tandem with steganography, another robust security technique comes into play - cryptography. Cryptography functions by transforming confidential data into an unreadable format, a process commonly known as encryption. Subsequently, decryption reverts the unreadable form back to its readable state. Frequently, users opt for either cryptography or steganography, but not both simultaneously. While cryptography stands as a potent security measure, the fusion of cryptography and steganography holds the potential to wield significant influence in this domain.
